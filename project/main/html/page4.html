Fun facts!

Fire and Ice: The ability to control fire and ice for cooking and storing food has been pivotal in human technological advancements.

Kitchen Accidents: The most common kitchen accident involves knife injuries, so always be careful when chopping.

Chef’s Attire: Ever wondered why chefs wear white? It’s because the chef of the first prime minister of France believed white symbolized hygiene1.

Chef’s Hat: The pleats in a chef’s hat are not just for show; they traditionally represented the number of recipes a chef had mastered1.

Lachanophobia: Yes, there’s a term for the fear of vegetables, and it’s called lachanophobia.

Some cooking hints & more fun facts

How to choose, sharpen, and use a good knife? The knife is important in cooking, so pay attention to its quality, sharpness, and safe use. A good knife is hard, sharp, comfortable, and of the right size. To sharpen the knife, use a sharpening tool and do it regularly. To use the knife, hold the handle properly and cut downward. When using the knife, be careful not to injure yourself and do not leave the knife in a dangerous place.

The characteristics, flavors, and recipes of the Mediterranean cuisine: The Mediterranean cuisine is the cuisine of the countries along the Mediterranean Sea, such as Italy, Spain, France, Greece, Turkey, Morocco, or Egypt. The characteristics of the Mediterranean cuisine are the fresh, natural, and varied ingredients, such as vegetables, fish, cheese, olive oil, spices, and herbs. The flavors of the Mediterranean cuisine are rich, aromatic, and tasty, which are given by rosemary, basil, garlic, lemon, wine, or balsamic vinegar. Some of the well-known recipes of the Mediterranean cuisine are pizza, pasta, paella, ratatouille, moussaka, hummus, tabbouleh, couscous, or baklava.
